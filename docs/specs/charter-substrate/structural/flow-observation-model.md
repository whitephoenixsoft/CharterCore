# Charter — Flow Observation Model

Status: FOUNDATIONAL (DRAFT)  
Applies to: CDS, VDS, CAS (indirect), CLI (inspection)  
Depends On: CDS (Deliberate System), CSG (Structure Graph), CCare Signal Model  
Does NOT define: signals, semantic states, enforcement rules, or telemetry systems  

---

# 1. Purpose

This document defines the **Flow Observation Model** for Charter.

It exists to:

- provide a consistent way to represent execution friction in the system  
- standardize how flow-related conditions are observed and recorded  
- support threshold-based signal generation in VDS  
- provide structured inputs that indirectly inform CAS  

Flow Observations are the bridge between:

- real-world system behavior  
- signal generation  
- semantic interpretation  

---

# 2. Core Principle

> Flow Observations describe how work moves through the system, not what it means.

Flow Observations must remain:

- descriptive  
- non-semantic  
- non-authoritative  
- structurally grounded  

---

# 3. Definition

A Flow Observation is:

> a structured record describing the movement, delay, or obstruction of work within the system.

It reflects:

- dependency traversal  
- execution progress  
- coordination friction  

---

# 4. Observation Scope

Flow Observations may apply to:

- a single resolution  
- a dependency relationship  
- a path of resolutions  
- a bounded subgraph (area)  

---

# 5. Observation Types

The system defines a minimal set of canonical Flow Observation types.

---

## 5.1 Blocked

Indicates that progress cannot continue due to an unmet dependency.

Properties may include:

- blocking dependency reference  
- start time of blockage  

---

## 5.2 Delayed

Indicates that progress is slower than expected but not fully blocked.

Properties may include:

- expected vs actual progression  
- duration of delay  

---

## 5.3 Stalled

Indicates no observable progress over a period of time.

Properties may include:

- last activity timestamp  
- duration of inactivity  

---

## 5.4 Reopened

Indicates that previously completed work has re-entered an active state.

Properties may include:

- number of reopen occurrences  
- time since last completion  

---

## 5.5 Bottleneck (Structural Observation)

Indicates concentration of dependency pressure at a node or small set of nodes.

Properties may include:

- number of incoming dependencies  
- degree of downstream impact  

---

# 6. Temporal Properties

All Flow Observations must include temporal context.

---

## 6.1 Start Time

When the condition began.

---

## 6.2 Duration

How long the condition has persisted.

---

## 6.3 Recurrence

Whether the condition has occurred repeatedly.

---

# 7. Relationship to Structure

Flow Observations must be grounded in CSG structure.

---

## 7.1 Dependency Awareness

Observations must reference:

- upstream dependencies  
- downstream impact where relevant  

---

## 7.2 Directionality

Flow Observations must respect dependency direction.

Blockage and delay propagate:

- forward along dependency paths  
- not backward  

---

# 8. Representation in CDS

Flow Observations are represented as:

> CDS Items using an observation subtype

---

## 8.1 Properties

Each observation item must include:

- type  
- target (resolution, relationship, or path)  
- temporal properties  
- optional structural references  

---

## 8.2 Behavior

Observation items:

- may be created manually or via VDS  
- may be marked as settled when resolved  
- do not create legitimacy  
- do not enforce action  

---

# 9. Relationship to VDS

VDS consumes Flow Observations to generate signals.

---

## 9.1 Thresholds

Thresholds may be defined over:

- blocker count  
- blocker duration  
- delay duration  
- recurrence frequency  
- bottleneck intensity  

---

## 9.2 Signal Generation

When thresholds are exceeded, VDS may emit signals such as:

- reduced_capacity  
- misalignment  
- need_reassessment  

---

## 9.3 Constraint

Flow Observations must not directly produce signals.

All signal generation must occur through VDS thresholds.

---

# 10. Relationship to CAS

CAS does not consume Flow Observations directly.

---

## 10.1 Indirect Influence

Flow Observations influence CAS through:

- signals generated by VDS  
- structural detection (e.g., bottlenecks)  

---

## 10.2 Conceptual Mapping

- persistent blockage → reduced capacity  
- prolonged delay → degraded flow  
- bottlenecks → structural strain  

---

# 11. Aggregation (Conceptual)

Flow Observations may be aggregated for analysis.

Examples:

- number of blocked items in a scope  
- average delay duration  
- concentration of bottlenecks  

Aggregation is:

- host-defined or VDS-defined  
- not part of the core observation model  

---

# 12. Constraints

Flow Observations must not:

- imply semantic meaning  
- assign blame  
- prescribe action  
- replace signals  
- replace CAS outputs  

---

# 13. Determinism

Given identical:

- observation inputs  
- structure references  
- temporal data  

Flow Observations must be consistent and reproducible.

---

# 14. Mental Model

Flow Observations answer:

- where work is getting stuck  
- where progress is slowing  
- where pressure is building  

They do not answer:

- why it is happening  
- what it means  
- what should be done  

---

# 15. Final Principle

Flow Observations ground the system in reality.

They ensure that:

- execution friction is visible  
- delays are measurable  
- structural pressure is observable  

They are the first step in transforming real-world behavior into:

- signals  
- semantic states  
- system understanding
